# Bridging TAIDL semantics to Gemmini IP-level energy in ACT

This report specifies the hardware-interface layer that connects TAIDL instruction semantics — decomposed into XLA-HLO primitives — to per-IP energy/latency cost on UC Berkeley's Gemmini accelerator, for use as an in-compiler cost oracle inside the ACT backend generator. **The contract you are filling is ACT's `cost_model(asm) → scalar` slot**, but at finer granularity: per HLO primitive, per Gemmini realization, summed up into per-TAIDL-instruction cost. The three TAIDL instructions in scope are `gemm` (`bf16[n,64] = dot(A_spad, B_spad) → acc`), `softmax` (rowwise over 64 columns, in/out in `acc`), and `gemm_acc` (`A·B + C` with `C` from `acc`). Two facts dominate the modeling: **default Gemmini supports neither `exp` nor `÷` in hardware** — softmax decomposes through Rocket fallback or, with `has_normalizations=true`, through the I-BERT polynomial unit — and **DAC '21 publishes only area, not pJ/MAC**, so absolute energy coefficients have to be filled from Horowitz/Timeloop proxies until you re-synthesize. ## Section 1 — Per-primitive realization enumeration on Gemmini

The XLA-HLO primitives that appear across `gemm`, `softmax`, and `gemm_acc` are: **`dot`**, **`add`**, **`exponential`**, **`reduce_add`**, **`broadcast`**, **`divide`**. ACT's IR-to-ISA rewrites already lower each TAIDL instruction's `g_θ(α)` body to one of these, so the interface layer's job is to enumerate Gemmini-resident realizations for each, plus a Rocket fallback marker. Below, every realization names the IP, the storage path, the dataflow variant, and the trigger condition. The bf16 caveat from Section 2 applies throughout: the canonical 16×16 default Gemmini config is INT8; bf16 today is the 8×8 `BF16DefaultConfig`.

### `dot` — the systolic array's home turf
`dot` has four meaningfully distinct realizations. **WS-mesh dot with B preloaded** is the default for `gemm`/`gemm_acc`: B (64×64) is preloaded into the array via `matmul.preload`, A (n×64) streams along rows from `spad`, partial sums leave the mesh into the 32-bit accumulator, and the accumulator's adder bank does the inner-product reduction across the K=4 sub-tiles (Gemmini's mesh is DIM=16 wide, the 64-deep contracting dim is decomposed into four 16-wide MAC blocks). This is selected when both operands live in `spad` and B is reusable across many A-rows — exactly the `gemm` shape. **OS-mesh dot with D preloaded** preloads C/bias into the PEs (using accumulator address as `D` via `matmul.preload`), streams A and B both, and right-shifts the PE-resident result on drain via `config_ex.rs2[31:0]`; the **transposer is automatically engaged on A** in OS mode because of how `spad` rows align. OS becomes attractive when the same A is consumed once and the bias/D term is handy in the accumulator — i.e., it's a candidate for `gemm_acc` when minimizing accumulator round-trip dominates, though in practice WS+accumulator-write-back is what the `gemmini-rocc-tests` library uses. **`im2col`-on-the-fly + WS dot** wraps the load path with the im2col module so a convolution flows as a GEMM without materializing the lowered matrix; not relevant for the three instructions here (no convolution), but the realization layer should still know about it because ACT-generated convolution paths will reach it. **`LoopMatmul` CISC-driven dot** is the same WS or OS path, but loop-control is performed by the `LoopMatmul` FSM rather than by Rocket emitting one RoCC at a time; same energy/cycle cost on the data path, lower instruction-issue overhead on the host, and it is enabled by default (`has_loop_matmul = true`).

### `add` — three different IPs, three very different costs
`add` resolves to **the accumulator's built-in 32-bit adder bank** when one operand is the running sum already in `acc` and the other is the systolic-array output (this is what makes `gemm_acc` essentially free: 16 parallel 32-bit adds per cycle, gated by bit 30 of the local Gemmini address — `1` = accumulate, `0` = overwrite). It resolves to **a degenerate matmul against a constant identity** (`add(A,B) = matmul([A;B], stack(I,I))` style with a const-tensor pre-emitted into HBM by ACT's compile-time-constant detector) when both operands are in `spad` and are not paired with a matmul; this is the case ACT documents in its arXiv Fig. 17 and reports a 6.82× speedup over Rocket. It falls to **Rocket scalar add** only if neither operand can be staged into accumulator/spad without exceeding a tile budget. For our three instructions, `add` only appears inside `gemm_acc` and there it is **always the accumulator-adder realization**.

### `exponential` — no native hardware in default config
This is the single most consequential cost-modeling fact. Default Gemmini's post-processing path supports ReLU/ReLU6, power-of-2 right-shift, float32 scale-multiply, saturation, and max-pool — but **no transcendentals**. Therefore `exp` has exactly two realizations: **Rocket-CPU fallback**, the default, in which the row leaves Gemmini through DRAM and is computed scalar on the host (this is what dominates BERT inference time — FullStack '23 reports >96% of runtime here); and **the I-BERT integer-polynomial datapath inside the optional normalization unit** (gated by `has_normalizations=true` and exposed via `config_norm`), a 16-wide bank of polynomial pipelines reading directly from the accumulator with bounded approximation error. The selection rule for the realization layer is purely a config check: if the target Gemmini config has `has_normalizations=true`, route `exp` to the normalization unit; otherwise mark it Rocket-fallback (out-of-scope for costed realizations per your scope statement, but the realization layer must still emit a "fallback" marker so cost summation degrades gracefully).

### `reduce_add` — accumulator sweep or constant-vector matmul
With **`has_normalizations=true`**, `reduce_add` along an accumulator-resident row is a **multi-pass sweep through the accumulator's reduction unit** (sum, sum-of-squares, max are all that's hardware-supported per the I-BERT extension); cost is essentially `ceil(W/16) * acc_latency` cycles per pass. Without the normalization unit, the canonical ACT-style realization is **`matmul(x, ones_vector)` on the systolic array**, with `ones_vector` emitted as a compile-time constant by ACT's pii-graph rewriter (also reported with a 6.88× speedup over Rocket in the ACT arXiv paper). Selection: if data is already in `acc` and `has_normalizations=true`, prefer the sweep — it is on-chip and avoids the round-trip; otherwise emit the const-MAC.

### `broadcast` — usually a no-op or a const-MAC
Gemmini has no dedicated broadcast unit. Two realizations: **`matmul(ones, x)`** via the systolic array with a const `ones` tile (the ACT arXiv reports this is only 1.04× over Rocket, i.e., barely worth offloading), and **address-arithmetic-only "pseudo-broadcast"** when the broadcast is consumed in-place by the next op (e.g., the `divide` step of softmax) and the consumer can read the same accumulator row repeatedly — effectively free in cycles, only the consumer's reads count. For softmax inside a fused on-chip path, the second realization dominates: the broadcast of the per-row sum to all 64 columns is just "the divider reads the same scalar 64 times," not a separate compute event.

### `divide` — no native hardware in default config
Same story as `exp`. **Rocket fallback** in default; **I-BERT i-Softmax-style power-of-2 shifter-as-divider** in the normalization unit. I-BERT specifically chooses the softmax denominator's normalization to be power-of-2 so that the "division" lowers to a barrel shifter, not a real divider — this is why the unit is cheap and why softmax in I-BERT mode costs roughly the same as `reduce_add`. Selection mirrors `exp`: gated entirely by `has_normalizations`.

A compact view of the realization table:

| Primitive | Realization | IP block | Storage path | Dataflow variant | Selected when |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `dot` | WS-mesh + acc reduce | Systolic array (16×16) + 32-bit acc-adder bank | spad→array→acc | weight-stationary | both ops in spad, B reused (gemm/gemm_acc) |
| `dot` | OS-mesh + transposer | Spatial array + transposer + PE-resident psum | spad→array→acc(scaled) | output-stationary, A transposed automatically | small reuse, on-the-fly bias add |
| `dot` | im2col + WS-mesh | im2col module + array | spad→im2col→array→acc | WS, on-the-fly conv lowering | conv lowering (not in our 3 instructions) |
| `dot` | LoopMatmul CISC | Same as above + LoopMatmul FSM | same | WS/OS | reduce host issue overhead |
| `add` | Acc adder bank | Accumulator built-in 32-bit adders (16 parallel) | (array out) ⊕ acc | n/a | C input lives in acc (gemm_acc) |
| `add` | Const-MAC | Systolic array w/ const identity tile | spad→array→acc | WS | both ops in spad, no fused matmul |
| `exp` | Norm unit polynomial | I-BERT activation bank (16-wide) on acc-read port | acc→norm→acc | n/a | `has_normalizations=true` |
| `exp` | Rocket fallback | Host CPU | acc→DRAM→Rocket→DRAM→acc | n/a | default config (out of scope to cost) |
| `reduce_add` | Acc reduction sweep | I-BERT reduction unit | acc→reduction→acc | multi-pass | `has_normalizations=true`, op in acc |
| `reduce_add` | Const-MAC `(x, ones)` | Systolic array | spad→array→acc | WS | otherwise, op in spad |
| `broadcast` | Address aliasing | (no IP — consumer reads scalar repeatedly) | acc-resident | n/a | consumer is on-chip and reads in place |
| `broadcast` | Const-MAC `(ones, x)` | Systolic array | spad→array→acc | WS | broadcast must be materialized (rare) |
| `divide` | Norm unit shifter | I-BERT activation bank (power-of-2 shifter) | acc→norm→acc | n/a | `has_normalizations=true` |
| `divide` | Rocket fallback | Host CPU | acc→DRAM→Rocket→DRAM→acc | n/a | default config |

DMA/transposer/im2col/Rocket appear in this table only as helper IPs; per your scope, **Rocket fallback is flagged but not costed in detail** in this pass.

## Section 2 — Per-realization cost formulas with grounded numbers

All formulas use symbolic per-IP energy knobs so coefficients can be filled later from a Horowitz-style proxy table or your own re-synthesis. Where Gemmini publishes a number, it is cited inline; where it doesn't (the common case for energy), the formula uses a symbolic knob and the substitution proxy is named.

**Symbol set.** `E_mac` = energy per single MAC (INT8 default; bf16 ≈ 4× per Horowitz/Sze); `E_acc_add` = energy per 32-bit accumulator add; `E_sa_pe_idle` = leakage per PE per cycle (sometimes worth modeling for low-utilization tiles); `E_sfu` = energy per element through the I-BERT polynomial pipeline (one `exp` evaluation); `E_reduce` = energy per element passed through the reduction unit; `E_div` = energy per element through the I-BERT shifter-divide; `E_xpose` = energy per element through the transposer; `e_byte_dram` = pJ/byte for DRAM (~20 pJ/B LPDDR4, ~5 pJ/B HBM2 — Berkeley papers use these as proxies, not as Gemmini measurements); `e_byte_spad` = pJ/byte for scratchpad SRAM; `e_byte_acc` = pJ/byte for accumulator SRAM. Cycle constants taken from `GemminiConfigs.scala`: `DIM=16`, `spad_read_delay=4`, `acc_latency=2`, `dma_buswidth=128 bits`, `dma_maxbytes=64 B`, `mvin_scale_args.latency=4`, `acc_scale_args.latency=8`, `tlb_size=4`, `max_in_flight_mem_reqs=16`.

### Systolic array `dot` — WS, n×64 by 64×64
Decompose into DIM-tiles: `I = ceil(n/16)`, `K = 4`, `J = 4`. Each output tile aggregates K MAC-block contributions through the accumulator adder. Steady-state throughput is one DIM×DIM matmul tile per `DIM` cycles after a `~3·DIM` fill/drain.

Cycles: **`Cycles_dot_WS(n) = DIM·(I·K·J) + 3·DIM = 256·ceil(n/16) + 48`**.

Energy (compute-only): **`E_dot_WS(n) = (n·64·64)·E_mac + (I·K·J·DIM·DIM)·E_acc_add + bytes_spad·e_byte_spad + bytes_acc·e_byte_acc`**, with `bytes_spad = (n·64 + 64·64)·sz_in` (A and B reads, `sz_in=1` for INT8, `2` for bf16) and `bytes_acc = I·K·J·DIM·DIM·4` (32-bit partial-sum writes per inner step). For the n=16, INT8 default: `Cycles ≈ 304`, `MACs = 65536`, achieved utilization ≈ 86% of the 256 MAC/cycle peak; DAC '21 reports ~74% averaged across realistic kernels.

### Systolic array `dot` — OS variant
OS adds an unavoidable transposer fill on A (DIM cycles) and applies the right-shift on drain rather than a separate scale.

Cycles: **`Cycles_dot_OS(n) = Cycles_dot_WS(n) + DIM`** (the transposer fill).
Energy: **`E_dot_OS(n) = E_dot_WS(n) + (n·64)·E_xpose - (I·K·J·DIM·DIM)·E_acc_add + (I·K·J·DIM·DIM)·E_shift`**, since OS replaces the per-inner-step accumulator add with an in-PE register chain and a final right-shift+saturate. `E_shift` is small (≪ `E_acc_add`).

### Accumulator-adder `add` (the gemm_acc bias path)
Cycles: **`Cycles_acc_add(n) = 0`** in the steady-state — the add is overlapped with each tile's commit cycle, gated by bit 30 of the destination address.
Energy: **`E_acc_add_total(n) = (n·64)·E_acc_add`** for the C ⊕ A·B accumulation (one 32-bit add per output element, all hidden under matmul cycles), plus the `mvin` energy for loading C into the accumulator at the start.

### DMA mvin/mvout
Cycles per transfer: **`Cycles_dma(B) ≈ ceil(B / dma_buswidth_bytes) + setup`**, with `dma_buswidth_bytes = 16`, `setup ≈ TLB_miss_penalty + max_in_flight_pipe_fill` (~16 cycles default amortized).
Energy: **`E_dma(B) = B · e_byte_dram + B · e_byte_spad`** (or `e_byte_acc` if mvin destination is acc), plus `B · E_mvin_scale` if the optional float32 mvin-scale unit is engaged (`mvin_scale_args.latency=4`, `Float(8,24)` mul).

### ReLU / scaling unit on the accumulator-read port (default capabilities only)
Cycles per element through the scale+activation: hidden under mvout/acc-read pipelining at 1 element per accumulator port per cycle, with `acc_scale_args.latency=8` of fixed pipeline depth.
Energy: **`E_scale_relu(n_elems) = n_elems · (E_fp32_mul + E_relu)`**, both small; in DAC '21 this unit is bundled into "post-processing" with no separate breakdown. Crucially, **this unit cannot do `exp` or `÷`** — see below.

### I-BERT normalization unit (when `has_normalizations=true`)
This is the only on-chip path for `exp`, `reduce_add`-on-acc, and `÷`. It is 16-wide, sits on the accumulator-read port, and runs polynomial pipelines.

Per-element through `exp`: **`E_exp_perelem ≈ E_sfu`** (a polynomial of small degree; FullStack '23 reports +14% area / +9.3% inference power total for the entire normalization extension at ASAP7).
Per-pass over a W-element row: **`Cycles_norm_pass(W) = ceil(W/16) · acc_latency + poly_pipe_latency ≈ ceil(W/16)·2 + 8`**.
Softmax row of width 64 (3 passes — max, Σexp, normalize): **`Cycles_softmax_row(64) ≈ 3·(4·2 + 8) = 48`** cycles.
Energy: **`E_softmax_row(64) = 64·E_sfu + 64·E_reduce + 64·E_div + 64·sz·e_byte_acc·n_passes`** with `n_passes=3` and `sz` the on-chip element width.

### Rocket fallback (out-of-scope to cost in detail)
The realization layer must emit a fallback flag; for completeness, the cost is on the order of `~200 cycles/element` for a scalar fp `exp` plus DRAM round-trips of the entire row, dwarfing any on-chip alternative. FullStack '23 attributes ~96% of BERT runtime to this when the normalization unit is absent — the most important practical signal that softmax cost in default-config Gemmini is a pathological case.

### Grounded numbers (what is and isn't published)
Gemmini's own DAC '21 paper publishes **areas (Intel 22FFL): spatial-array 116 K µm², scratchpad 256 KB → 544 K µm², accumulator 64 KB → 146 K µm², total ≈1.03 mm²**, and **performance** (ResNet-50 22.8 FPS, 2670× over in-order Rocket; BERT 144× without the norm unit). It does **not** publish absolute pJ/MAC or pJ/byte — `E_mac`, `e_byte_spad`, `e_byte_acc`, `e_byte_dram` must come from Horowitz ISSCC '14 / Sze textbook proxies (FullStack '23 §3.3 confirms these are exactly the inputs Berkeley feeds Timeloop), or from re-synthesis. The I-BERT extension publishes **+14% area / +9.3% power / 39.6× BERT speedup** at ASAP7 — adequate to set `E_sfu`, `E_reduce`, `E_div` ratios relative to `E_mac` once you fix `E_mac` from the proxy.

## Section 3 — Worked end-to-end derivations for the three instructions

The interface layer for each instruction does the same five-step lowering: (1) destructure `g_θ(α)` into HLO primitives, (2) check fused-IP patterns, (3) assign realizations per primitive given operand storage, (4) sum cycles and energy across realizations, (5) add memory-traffic terms `B_total · e_byte_*` for any data movement that the realization layer didn't already absorb. The fusion check answers a single question first: **does Gemmini have a fused IP for this pattern?** For default config: no fused softmax, no fused matmul-bias-relu beyond what OS dataflow + scale-relu already does, no fused QK·softmax·V. With `has_normalizations=true`: a quasi-fused softmax (the three passes are issued back-to-back and never leave the accumulator). Newer research extensions (Softermax, VEXP at the core side, the Autocomp tuner) do not exist in upstream Gemmini and should not be considered fused for this writeup.

### `gemm`: bf16[n,64] = dot(bf16[n,64], bf16[64,64]), spad×spad→acc
Decomposition: a single `dot` primitive with `lhs_contracting={1}, rhs_contracting={0}`. Realization: **WS-mesh dot with B preloaded** (Section 1). Fusion: none beyond the implicit accumulator-adder reduction across K=4. Closed-form:

**`Cycles_gemm(n) = 256·ceil(n/16) + 48`**

**`E_gemm(n) = n·64·64·E_mac  +  ceil(n/16)·4·4·DIM·DIM·E_acc_add  +  B_gemm·e_byte`**

with `B_gemm = (n·64 + 64·64)·sz_bf16 + n·64·sz_bf16` covering A/B spad reads and C accumulator writes (and a rescale to bf16 on mvout if `acc_scale_args` is engaged). Sanity check for n=64: 262 144 MACs, 1072 cycles, 244 MAC/cycle achieved — close to the 74% real-world utilization target.

### `softmax`: rowwise over 64 columns, in/out in `acc`
Decomposition (matches the slide-deck formula and your TAIDL definition): `%a = exp(%In)`; `%r = reduce_add(%a, dim=1)`; `%b = broadcast(%r, dim=0)`; `ROOT = divide(%a, %b)`. Note this is the **un-stabilized softmax** — no `reduce_max` subtract — because the TAIDL spec you gave doesn't include it. The numerically-stable version that ACT actually compiles for transformer attention adds a `reduce_max` and a `subtract` first; flag this as a near-term TAIDL hardening item.

Fusion check: **default config — no fused softmax IP**. Softmax falls to Rocket and the realization layer should emit a single Rocket-fallback marker for the entire row plus DRAM round-trip energy. **With `has_normalizations=true` — quasi-fused** I-BERT softmax: `exp`, `reduce_add`, and `÷` all run inside the normalization unit on the same accumulator residency, in three passes; `broadcast` is realized as **address aliasing** (consumer reads the per-row sum as a scalar 64 times — zero compute, only 64 acc-reads). Closed-form, I-BERT path, per row:

**`Cycles_softmax_row(64) ≈ 3·(ceil(64/16)·acc_latency + poly_pipe_latency) ≈ 3·(8+8) = 48`**

**`E_softmax_row(64) = 64·(E_sfu + E_reduce + E_div) + B_softmax · e_byte_acc`**

with `B_softmax = 64·sz_bf16·3` (three passes over the 64-element row — note the realization layer must accumulate this even though the row never leaves `acc`, because acc-internal reads are not free). For a softmax over `n` rows: **`E_softmax(n) = 64·n·(E_sfu + E_reduce + E_div) + B_total · e_byte`** — exactly the slide-deck formula, with `B_total = 3·64·n·sz_bf16` and `e_byte = e_byte_acc`. The realization layer should emit a parameterized version that swaps in `e_byte_dram + scalar_softmax_cycles_per_row · cycles_to_energy(rocket)` when `has_normalizations=false`, with a clear "uncosted-fallback" flag.

### `gemm_acc`: A·B + C, spad×spad + acc → acc
Decomposition: `dot(A, B)` followed by `add(C, dot_result)`. Realization: **WS-mesh dot with B preloaded + accumulator-adder bank for C** (gated by destination-address bit 30 = 1, as in `gemmini_extended_compute_accumulated`). Fusion: this **is the canonical fused pattern on Gemmini** — the C add is overlapped with the matmul commit, costing zero additional cycles. The only delta vs. plain `gemm` is (a) one extra mvin to bring C into `acc` if it isn't already there (frequently it is, because C is the previous tile's output), and (b) `n·64` extra 32-bit accumulator adds, all hidden under matmul cycles.

**`Cycles_gemm_acc(n) = Cycles_gemm(n)`** if C already lives in `acc`; **`+ Cycles_dma(n·64·sz_bf16)`** otherwise.

**`E_gemm_acc(n) = E_gemm(n) + n·64·E_acc_add  +  [C-resident? 0 : (n·64·sz_bf16) · (e_byte_dram + e_byte_acc)]`**

In other words: **`gemm_acc` ≈ `gemm` + `n·64·E_acc_add` ≈ `gemm` + ε**. The accumulator's built-in adder bank is essentially free in cycles and small in energy compared with `n·64·64·E_mac`, which is one of Gemmini's nicer architectural properties for transformer attention's `S = QK^T + bias` step.

## Section 4 — Ground-truth validation methodology (compute-only, first pass)

The objective is to bound the percent-error of `E_estimator(instr) - E_groundtruth(instr)` per TAIDL instruction across realistic shapes and to verify that the *ranking* of dominant IPs matches between estimator and ground truth (this matters more than absolute magnitude for ASIC DSE: as long as the estimator agrees on which IP to optimize first, the compiler can drive design exploration sensibly even if absolute energy is off by a constant).

### Configurations to pin
Lock the comparison to two Gemmini configs from `Configs.scala`, both at default DIM=16 (or 8 for the bf16 variant) with `has_loop_matmul=true`, im2col off, no clock gating: a **default integer 16×16** (`GemminiRocketConfig` with `Dataflow.WS`, SP=256 KB, ACC=64 KB) for the `gemm`/`gemm_acc` baseline, and a **bf16 8×8 with normalizations** (`BF16DefaultConfig` derivative with `has_normalizations=true`, SP=64 KB, ACC=256 KB — the FullStack '23 transformer-tuned shape) for `softmax`. Pinning two configs (CNN-tuned and Transformer-tuned) is enough to surface whether the estimator's per-IP ranking is configuration-agnostic.

### Ground-truth energy: three options, ranked
**Option A (recommended for first pass): Verilator RTL simulation + per-toggle activity-based energy lookup.** Compile Gemmini through the standard Chipyard flow (`make CONFIG=GemminiRocketConfig verilog`), simulate workloads under Verilator with VCD dumping enabled, then feed activity counts into a per-IP energy table you build by re-synthesizing each IP standalone (the Chipyard ASIC flow with a Cadence/Synopsys library or with the open ASAP7/SkyWater130 PDK). This yields per-cycle, per-IP energy traces that align cleanly with the realization layer's IPs. **Option B: published per-op characterization.** Use the DAC '21 area numbers + FullStack '23 ASSYST percentages directly, scaled by per-cycle utilization from a cycle-accurate simulation; cheaper but rougher. **Option C: post-synthesis power simulation.** Synthesize the full Gemmini in a known PDK (ASAP7 is what the I-BERT paper uses; Intel 22FFL is what DAC '21 used and is unavailable to most), run gate-level power simulation with PrimeTime PX or Innovus's signoff power; the ground truth here is most accurate but the workflow is heaviest. Pick **A for the first pass** and migrate to C once estimator error stabilizes.

### Workloads per instruction
For `gemm`: shapes `n ∈ {16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512}` with the 64×64 B fixed, plus four shapes from the gemmini-rocc-tests `template_matmul` family for cross-check. For `softmax`: a sweep of `n ∈ {16, 64, 256, 1024}` row-counts at width=64, plus the `softmax` kernel from the FullStack '23 BERT decomposition; the comparison must be done **separately** under `has_normalizations=true` and `false`, because the realizations are entirely different IPs and the ground-truth energy will differ by orders of magnitude. For `gemm_acc`: same `n` sweep as `gemm`, but with an `mvin` of C preceding the matmul, executed twice — once with C already resident (the realistic transformer case) and once with C from DRAM — so the realization layer's storage-locality branch gets exercised. Each workload should run for at least a few thousand cycles of steady state to wash out fill/drain transients.

### Comparison protocol
For each (workload, instruction) pair, compute three quantities. **(i) Per-IP energy share**: percentage of total energy per IP in the estimator vs ground truth — this is the most diagnostic comparison because it directly tells you whether `E_mac` is too high relative to `E_acc_add`, etc. **(ii) Total compute energy** (the scalar your `cost_model(asm)` returns): compute `(E_est - E_gt)/E_gt` per workload, report mean and worst-case absolute percent error and a Pearson correlation across workloads. **(iii) IP-ranking agreement**: Spearman rank correlation between the estimator's and ground truth's "which IP burns the most energy per workload" ordering. Target thresholds for first pass: ≤25% mean absolute error, ≤50% worst-case, Spearman ≥0.8 — generous bounds, but the compiler's design-exploration use case tolerates a constant-factor offset as long as the relative ordering is right. As you refine the energy knobs (e.g., re-synthesizing PE area to get a tighter `E_mac`), tighten to ≤10%/≤20%/≥0.95.

### Compute-only scope (and how to keep storage hooks live)
For this pass, ground truth is **compute-only**: synthesize and toggle-count the spatial array, accumulator-adder bank, transposer, and (when present) normalization unit. Do **not** feed SRAM/DRAM activity into the ground-truth energy aggregate. The estimator, however, retains its `B_total · e_byte_*` terms in the formula; you simply set `e_byte_spad = e_byte_acc = e_byte_dram = 0` for the comparison. This keeps the architectural capability live: the moment you have a defensible storage-energy ground-truth pipeline (e.g., CACTI-derived SRAM energies + a DRAM model like DRAMPower), you flip the knobs back on and rerun the same comparison harness without code changes. Document this as a **two-flag config**: `cost_storage_energy ∈ {off, on}`, `gt_storage_energy ∈ {off, on}` — only the (off, off) and (on, on) corners are scientifically meaningful. ## Conclusion

The hardware-interface layer's job is small but load-bearing: it owns the realization choice for each HLO primitive given the active Gemmini config, and it owns the symbolic-to-numeric energy bridge. The three TAIDL instructions illustrate three regimes of Gemmini cost modeling — **`gemm` is the systolic-array sweet spot** (closed-form, dominated by `E_mac`, well-characterized), **`gemm_acc` is essentially `gemm` plus a free accumulator add** (the bit-30 trick is the IP-fusion story Gemmini already gives you), and **`softmax` is the cliff** (no native hardware in default config; either Rocket pathology or a quasi-fused I-BERT polynomial path). Validation should be compute-only with storage hooks live but disabled, ranked by per-IP share rather than absolute total, and the immediate value is unlocking ASIC DSE inside the ACT compile loop — long before storage modeling and operator coverage catch up. The two largest residual risks are (a) the Horowitz proxy for `E_mac` being far enough off in 22FFL that absolute-error bounds blow out (mitigated by re-synthesis) and (b) the un-stabilized `softmax` TAIDL spec masking the cost of `reduce_max` (mitigated by a one-line TAIDL hardening in the next sprint). Neither blocks the first pass.
